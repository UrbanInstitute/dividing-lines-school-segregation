Where School Boundaries Segregate Black and Hispanic Students

This tool highlights racially unequal school boundaries nationwide, which we define as boundaries that separate schools with a 25 percentage-point difference in representation of Black or Hispanic households and in the representation of white households. The map shows the race and ethnicity of all households in the districts displayed, whereas the charts show the share of households by race and ethnicity within 500 meters of the boundary. The charts also display school pairs in ranked order, starting with the most unequal boundary in each metro area, and indicate whether a boundary separates schools within one school district or across two districts.
